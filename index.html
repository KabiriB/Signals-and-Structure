Approach

How the work thinks

Across different formats, the work follows a consistent method: read institutions structurally, compare variation carefully, and translate evidence into accessible explanation.

Structure-first

Read systems before blaming individuals

I treat people as sensors of institutional conditions rather than as isolated explanations. Variation in trust, pressure, and behaviour often points to structure before it points to character.

Comparative

Use difference to reveal hidden structure

Comparing neighbourhoods, wards, cities, and institutional settings helps reveal where governance ecologies diverge and where surface similarity hides deeper variation.

Explanation

Turn evidence into clearer judgment

The goal is not only analysis, but explanation: making complex evidence legible to scholars, editors, collaborators, and decision-makers without flattening its depth.

About

Scholarship and analysis

I am a senior urban researcher and analyst working at the intersection of migration, governance, and decision intelligence.

My academic work examines how mobility, uncertainty, and institutional fragmentation reshape civic life, belonging, and political engagement in African cities. Trained in migration studies and grounded in comparative urban research, I combine survey analysis, spatial methods, and conceptual inquiry to ask how institutions actually function under real-world constraints.

Alongside peer-reviewed research, I write public essays and applied briefs that translate complex evidence into clearer, more responsible decision-making. This space is designed to hold both sides of that work: scholarly inquiry and practical diagnosis.

This site is for

Scholars, collaborators, editors, policy thinkers, and decision-makers who want a clearer view of how institutions, incentives, and urban realities interact.
