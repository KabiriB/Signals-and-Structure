# Signals-and-Structure
Structure-first analysis of governance and institutional variation, integrating political theory, comparative data, and executable models.

About

Signals and Structure is a hybrid research lab exploring governance as observable signal.

The core premise is simple: political outcomes are not random events. They are patterned expressions of institutional structure. Citizens, organisations, and local conflicts generate signals — trust levels, protest events, service breakdowns, mobility flows — that can be read as indicators of how institutions are functioning beneath the surface.

This repository brings together:

Working papers and methodological notes

Cleaned datasets and replication files

Comparative governance analyses

Applied decision intelligence tools and dashboards

The work moves between theory and execution. Political concepts are treated seriously, but they are also operationalised. Methods are transparent. Assumptions are stated plainly. Where data is limited, limits are acknowledged.

The focus is comparative and institutional — with particular attention to governance variation across contexts, especially in African urban settings.

The aim is not prediction for its own sake. The aim is diagnostic clarity: to read structure through signal, and to make institutional variation legible.
